I spent the summer of 2012 with the Division of Clinical Anatomy at Stanford University splitting time working with fresh human cadavers and performing research in a cutting edge stem cell and regenerative medicine laboratory.

In the former, I studied human anatomy, physiology, and pathology in the most interactive way possible: through surgical demonstrations on human bodies. This concluded with an iBook that blended topics on the anatomy and physiology of the human heart, which you can check out in a most riveting presentation below (disclaimer: sarcasm).

For the second part of the internship, I worked in the research lab of Jill Helms, Professor of Plastic & Reconstructive Surgery at Stanford University. There, my team examined skeletal tissue repair in the presence of oral implants ("osseointegration"), a study which was published in 2014.
